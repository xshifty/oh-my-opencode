How You Use OpenCode

Why This Matters: OpenCode gives you many tools (read, write, bash, edit, grep, glob, and more). How you use them tells a story about your workflow. If you use bash a lot, you rely on the command line. If you read much more than you write, you spend most of your time exploring before building. Error rates show where things go wrong — for example, many write errors often mean you are trying to save files to paths that do not exist yet. Fixing these patterns can make your sessions much smoother.

Response Time Distribution

Why This Matters: Response time measures how fast the agent replies to you. Short times mean quick, smooth conversations. Long times (over 5 minutes) suggest the agent is working on something complex — or it got stuck. If you see a big gap between the median and average, that means most responses are fast but a few take very long, which can break your flow. The goal is to keep most responses in the fast buckets.
